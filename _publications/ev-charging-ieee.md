**Status:** Manuscript in preparation for *IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid*<br>
**Context:** Collaboration with a real EV charging operator serving European customers

This work calibrates each vehicle's natural AC charging envelope and schedules mixed single- and three-phase charging sessions under aggregate and per-phase grid constraints.

The primary operational metric is **service stretch**: actual residence time relative to the vehicle's standalone charging time. The scheduling problem seeks work-conserving, grid-compliant allocations that minimize congestion-induced stretch while respecting vehicle charging curves and phase-specific capacity.

The current policy direction is phase-aware shortest effective remaining time, supported by real-session calibration and event-based simulation.
